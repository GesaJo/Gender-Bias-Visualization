Enter a couple of words to start a sentence and the model will finish the sentence for you. If the sentence has a subject that is male or female, you will also get the finished sentence for the other binary gender to compare word-choices and topics. Even if sometimes the model doesn't make a lot of sense, it is interesting to see which words are chosen in the female and the male version of the sentence.
If there is no gendered subject, you'll get another possible ending for the sentence. I becomes apparent that some phrases are strongly connected to a gender, for example "the great person was" is always finished (at least in a sample of 20 runs) with a neutral or a male object.

As there is a little randomness to escape loops, it can be useful to run the same words a couple of times.

Model: GPT2, trained on 8 million webpages.
How it works well:
  • Use min. 3 words
  • results for capitalized words are different than lowercase
