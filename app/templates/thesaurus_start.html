Give me more words like:







This tool returns synonyms for the given words. Those synonyms are coloured if they are usually used in a male (red) or female (blue) context, the more biased they are, the lighter the colour.
It becomes apparent that its not only unique words that are used in certain gendered contexts, but sometimes whole "concepts", for example all but one word returned for "genius" are either neutral or closer to man than to woman.
Also, the male-as-norm prinicple becomes apparent here: For example the synonyms for "man" include "humanity" and "human being", the ones for women don't. Likewise, the synonyms for "person" are all neutral or male biased.

Model: Wordnet (nltk).


How it works:
  • Use one word
  • Terms with more than one word can't be processed in the model that detects the bias, so they'll always appear neutral
  • Red: male
  • Blue: female

    • Examples:
  • genius
  • person
  • lovely
  • strong
  • boss
  • designer
